INDIA suffered a fresh wave of terror last night when an explosion destroyed two blocks of flats in the centre of the city of Calcutta killing at least 45 people and injuring scores of others
Police said a bomb exploded in the crowded trade and residential district of Bowbazaar shortly after midnight causing two blocks of flats to collapse and trapping up to 70 people in the rubble
A militant Sikh group fighting for independence for the Indian state of Punjab has claimed responsibility for the Bombay blasts but police are unconvinced saying they were carried out by a foreign group in connivance with 'local criminals'
Mr Shankarrao Chavan the federal home (interior) minister told parliament in New Delhi that he suspected 'an international conspiracy'
