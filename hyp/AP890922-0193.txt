Here is a chronology of Hurricane Hugo's destructive path across the Caribbean into South Carolina
Forecasters monitor new tropical wave forming off West Africa southwest of the Cape Verde Islands
Storm reaches the 39 mph tropical storm threshold and is named Hugo
Sept 13 _ Hugo develops into a hurricane as winds reach 74 mph
Storm is about 1000 miles east of the Lesser Antilles island chain
_ Hugo bears down on Guadeloupe and Dominica with winds reaching 140 mph
Five people are reported killed 84 injured and more than 15000 homeless on the island of 340000 people
Sept 17 _ Hugo hits British island of Montserrat
On the British Virgin Islands trees are uprooted and power and communications knocked out
Sailboats were blown out of the water and thrown up to 150 feet on shore in St Thomas
On St Croix widespread looting is reported including raids by machete-wielding gangs
_ Hugo crosses the northeast corner of Puerto Rico
On the Bahamas islanders boarded up homes and stocked up as Hugo moved westward
Sept 20 _ Headed toward the southeast coast of the United States Hugo has winds of 105-125 mph
Hugo crashes into Charleston SC with winds of 135 mph leveling buildings and flooding streets
