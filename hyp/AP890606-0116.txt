Here is a look at key events in the student pro-democracy movement the government crackdown and the fighting between rival army factions
Former Communist Party chief Hu Yaobang dies
Beijing University students put up posters praising him and indirectly criticizing opponents who forced his resignation after student demonstrations in 1986-87
_ Thousands of students march in Beijing and Shanghai expressing dissatisfaction with the government and shouting ``Long live Hu Yaobang
About 1000 stage a sit-in at the Great Hall of the People to demand greater democracy a free press and more money for education
Students begin boycott of university classes
April 29 _ Officials meet with student leaders but independent student groups are not satisfied and continue a class boycott at 41 universities
Demonstrations are held in Shanghai Nanjing and other cities
May 13 _ About 2000 students begin a hunger strike in Tiananmen Square
May 14 _ Thousands flock to the square to back the students
The government deadline for students to leave the square passes
Soviet President Mikhail S Gorbachev's welcoming ceremony planned near the square must be moved to the airport
Students reject his appeal and hold marches that generate 1 million supporters in Beijing
May 18 _ About 1 million people including many workers again take to the streets to show their support for the hunger strikers
A tearful Zhao visits weakened hunger strikers
Students resume their hunger strike then abandon it saying they will need their strength for the struggle ahead
At least one military convoy is reported to withdraw
Students unveil their ``Goddess of Democracy'' a replica of the Statue of Liberty on the square
Army reinforces positions in city center
